# Key Performance Indicators (KPIs)

## Overview

Key Performance Indicators (KPIs) transform raw sales data into meaningful business metrics that support decision-making.

The KPIs defined in PharmaVision BI are grouped according to their business purpose and the decisions they support.

---

# Executive KPIs

These indicators provide a high-level overview of commercial performance.

| KPI | Description | Business Question |
|------|-------------|-------------------|
| Total Revenue | Total sales revenue for the selected period | EQ-01 |
| Units Sold | Total units sold | EQ-01 |
| Year-over-Year Growth (%) | Revenue growth compared to the same period in the previous year | EQ-02 |
| Contribution (%) | Percentage contribution of each region or product to total revenue | EQ-03 |
| Active Customers | Number of customers with sales activity | EQ-05 |

---

# Commercial Performance KPIs

| KPI | Description | Business Question |
|------|-------------|-------------------|
| Revenue by Region | Revenue generated by each region | EQ-03 |
| Revenue by District | Revenue generated by each district | OQ-01 |
| Revenue by Route | Revenue generated by each sales route | OQ-02 |
| Revenue by Distributor | Revenue generated by each distributor | OQ-03 |
| Revenue by Product | Revenue generated by each product | OQ-04 |

---

# KPI Design Principles

All KPIs should:

- Be calculated automatically.
- Support at least one business question.
- Update dynamically according to dashboard filters.
- Be clearly defined and reproducible.
- Use consistent calculation logic.

# Why this matters

KPIs are the language of Business Intelligence.

Well-designed KPIs allow organizations to monitor performance, identify opportunities, and make informed decisions without manually analyzing raw data.

Every KPI included in PharmaVision BI has a direct relationship with a business objective and is designed to support actionable decision-making.
